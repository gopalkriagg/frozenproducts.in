Some extra knowledge about peas: Fresh peas vs Frozen peas

Peas' sugars turn into starch as soon as they are picked. After 24 hours, those sweet little green gems can be grainy and dull-tasting. Frozen peas, on the other hand, are picked and frozen at the peak of ripeness, halting the process of sugars turning to starch, so frozen peas are much better and will have the sweet taste you're looking for even after several months without any addition of preservatives! Plus, you don't have to do any podding! Generally, frozen vegetables/products retain more vitamins and seems to taste fresher. Frozen vegetables/products have advantages over fresh ones as the same are available when the fresh counterpart is out-of-season. They have practically infinite shelf life when kept in a freezer.

Knowledge on Recovery/yield

Fresh peas with pods have approximately 35% to 40% recovery i.e. for every 1 kg of pea pod you buy you will get only about 350 to 400 grams of peas so buying 1 kg of frozen peas is equivalent to buying 2.5 kg of peas with pods. This means that the cost and effort of using frozen peas is very less!

As long ago as 1959, sales of frozen peas in Britain overtook sales of pods, despite the fact that very few households owned a freezer at this point.

In India, still only about 10% of the peas consumption is of frozen peas and 90% of peas are consumed fresh. This is primarily due to unawareness in Indian consumers that frozen peas are as healthy, nutritious and sweet as freshly picked peas. Also they are much cheaper than peas with pods during off-season and don’t require any podding.
